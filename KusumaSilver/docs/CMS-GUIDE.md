# Editing the Kusuma Silver website

A guide for changing the words and pictures on the website yourself. You do not
need any technical knowledge, and you cannot break anything permanently — every
change can be undone.

---

## 1. Opening the content manager

Go to **kusumasilver.com/studio** and sign in.

You will see a menu down the left-hand side:

| Menu item | What is in it |
|---|---|
| **Website Pages** | The words and photos on each page — Home, The Catalogue, Our Story, Silver Class, Contact |
| **Jewellery** | Every piece you sell |
| **Categories** | The groups your pieces belong to — Rings, Necklaces, and so on |
| **Customer Orders** | Orders placed on the website (for looking at, not editing) |
| **Site Settings** | Your address, phone number, opening hours, the menu, and the footer |

---

## 2. Finding the page you want to change

Click **Website Pages**, then the page name.

Each page is split into **tabs** along the top — for example the Home page has
*Top of page*, *Section order*, *Catalogue strip*, *Heritage band*, *Manifesto*
and *Search & sharing*. Click a tab to jump to that part of the page.

If you are not sure which tab something is in, open the page on the website in
another window and work down it in order — the tabs follow the same order.

---

## 3. Changing text

Click into the box, type your change, and click **Publish** (bottom right).

Most text boxes have **two fields inside them**:

- **Bahasa Indonesia** — shown on the Indonesian site
- **English** — shown on the English site

You can leave English blank. If you do, the Indonesian text is shown on the
English site too, so nothing ever disappears.

**If you clear a box completely**, the website falls back to its original
built-in wording. That is a safety net — an empty box never leaves a blank space
on the page.

---

### Bullet points, numbering, bold and links

Longer paragraphs — a piece's **Description**, the story paragraphs on Our Story,
the intro on Silver Class, the home page paragraphs — have a small toolbar above
them.

To make a list (for example, explaining what a dragonfly motif means):

1. Click into the box and type your intro line, then press Enter.
2. Click the **bullet list** or **numbered list** button in the toolbar.
3. Type each point, pressing Enter between them.
4. To stop the list, press Enter twice.

You can also make text **bold** or *italic*, and add a link.

**There is no heading button, and that is deliberate.** The page design decides
what headings look like, so a heading typed here would not match the rest of the
site. If you need a new heading, it is a separate field — or ask your developer.

Short fields — page headings, button labels, the small labels above headings —
stay plain text on purpose. Formatting inside a button would break the layout.

## 4. Changing a photo

1. Click the photo you want to replace.
2. Click **Replace** (or drag a new image onto it).
3. Click **Publish**.

### Choosing which part of the photo stays visible

Photos are often trimmed to fit their space — a tall photo in a wide slot, for
example. You decide which part survives the trim:

1. Click the photo, then the **crop icon** (⤢) above it.
2. Drag the **circle** over the most important part — a face, a ring, a hand.
3. Drag the **dashed rectangle** if you also want to cut off an edge.
4. Click **Publish**.

The circle is the part the website keeps. If a photo looks badly cut off on the
website, this is what to adjust.

### Describing a photo

Every photo has a **Photo description** box. Write what is in the picture, for
example *"A silversmith shaping a ring over an open flame"*.

This matters for two reasons: visitors who cannot see images have it read aloud
to them, and Google uses it to understand your photos. Leave it blank and the
website uses the page heading instead.

### Choosing a photo's shape

Photos in the **Our Story** gallery have a **Photo shape** setting:

- **Square** — the standard
- **Landscape (wide)**
- **Portrait (tall)**
- **Original shape** — no trimming at all

Other photos on the site have a shape fixed by the design, so there is no
setting for them. That is deliberate — it keeps the pages looking right.

### Hiding a photo

Some photos have a **Hide this photo** switch. Turn it on to take the photo off
the website while keeping it here, ready to bring back later.

## 5. Adding a piece of jewellery

1. Click **Jewellery**, then the **＋** at the top.
2. Fill in at least: **Name (Indonesian)**, **Category**, **Price**, and one
   **Photo**, plus **Description (Indonesian)**.
3. Open the **Advanced** tab and check the **Page address** has filled itself in
   from the name. It normally does — you rarely need to touch it.
4. Click **Publish**.

Notes:

- **Price** is in Rupiah, digits only, no dots. Type `350000` to show
  *Rp 350.000*.
- The **first photo** is the one shown on the catalogue card. The next three
  appear as small pictures on the piece's own page.
- **Available to buy** off marks the piece unavailable and hides its Add to Bag
  button — use this instead of deleting a piece that is temporarily out of stock.
- **Show on the home page** puts the piece in the scrolling strip on the front page.
- Leave **Material**, **Lead time**, **Origin** and **Technique** blank unless
  this piece differs from your usual. Blank means "use the standard wording",
  which you set once in Site Settings.

---

## 5b. Managing the catalogue filters

The filters shoppers use — **Gemstone**, **Material**, **Ring size**, **Length** —
come from lists you control. Click **Filters** in the sidebar.

### Adding a new gemstone (or material, or size)

1. Click **Filters → Gemstones**.
2. Click **＋**, type the name, click **Publish**.
3. Open the piece it belongs to, and tick it under **Gemstones**.

**One stone per entry.** Write *Amethyst* on its own, not
"Amethyst, Garnet and Peridot". A piece can have as many as you like — tick them
all — and a shopper searching for any one of them will find it.

That is the whole point of these lists: the filter can only offer what is in
them, so a typo can never create a duplicate option like "Amethyst" and
"amethist" sitting side by side.

### Sizes come in two kinds

When adding a size you choose what kind it is:

- **Ring size** — 6, 7, 8 …
- **Length** — 45 cm, 50 cm …
- **One size / other**

They appear as *separate* filters, so someone browsing rings is never offered
"45 cm". Sizes are also what shoppers pick from on the piece page.

### The filters look after themselves

A filter only shows options that some piece actually uses, and it disappears
entirely when there is nothing to choose between. So you never have to tidy the
catalogue page — just keep the lists and the pieces accurate.

Drag any entry to change the order it appears in the dropdown.

## 5c. Prices and stock per gemstone or size

A piece has a **base price**, and each gemstone and each size can change it.

Open a piece, go to **Details**, and you will see **Gemstones** and **Sizes**.
Each row has three things:

- which gemstone or size it is
- **Price change** — how much it adds to the base price, in Rupiah
- **Available** — untick when that option runs out

**Example — a necklace that costs more the longer it is.** Base price
Rp 130.000, then:

| Size | Price change | Shopper pays |
|---|---|---|
| 45 cm | 0 | Rp 130.000 |
| 50 cm | 50000 | Rp 180.000 |
| 55 cm | 100000 | Rp 230.000 |
| 60 cm | 150000 | Rp 280.000 |

Type `50000`, not `50.000` — digits only, no dots. A negative number like
`-25000` makes an option cheaper.

**When something sells out**, untick **Available** on that row. Shoppers still
see the option, greyed out and crossed through, so they know the piece comes in
that size — they just cannot buy it today. Tick it again when it is back.

**On the catalogue**, a piece whose options cost different amounts shows
"From Rp 130.000", and its button says *Choose options* instead of adding
straight to the bag — so nobody accidentally buys the wrong stone.

## 6. Putting things in a different order

Anywhere you see a list, you can drag items into the order you want.

**To reorder pieces or categories:** click **Jewellery** or **Categories**. Grab
the **⠿ handle** on the left of a row and drag it up or down. The new order
saves by itself and is the order visitors see.

**To reorder items inside a page** — the steps on Silver Class, the values on Our
Story, the figures in the dark band, the cards in the catalogue strip, the menu
links — grab the **⠿ handle** on the left of the item and drag. Then click
**Publish**.

You never have to type numbers. The step numbers on the Silver Class page
(01, 02, 03…) renumber themselves automatically when you reorder them.

---

## 7. Changing the order of the sections on the home page

1. Open **Website Pages → Home**.
2. Click the **Section order** tab.
3. Drag the sections into the order you want.
4. Click **Publish**.

The top section with the big headline is always first and is not in this list.

To take a section off the page, click it open and turn on **Hide this section**.
Its content stays exactly as it is, ready for when you turn it back on.

If the list is empty, the page uses its standard order — that is fine and is how
it starts out.

---

## 8. Hiding something instead of deleting it

Almost everything has a **Hide this** switch: menu links, steps, values,
figures, photos, whole sections, and pieces of jewellery (**Available to buy**).

**Prefer hiding to deleting.** Hiding is instant to undo; deleting is not.

---

## 9. Changing your contact details, menu, or footer

These are all in **Site Settings**, and you only ever type them once — they
update everywhere on the site at the same time.

| Tab | What it changes |
|---|---|
| **Shop name & logo** | The shop name, the logo, the small line under it |
| **Contact & location** | Address, WhatsApp number, email, opening hours, the map |
| **Social media** | Instagram, TikTok, Facebook |
| **Menu** | The announcement bar at the very top, and the menu links |
| **Footer** | The footer paragraph, its link columns, and the copyright line |
| **Repeated text** | The standard Material / Origin / Technique / Lead time shown on every piece |

Two things worth knowing:

- The **WhatsApp number** powers every WhatsApp button on the site. It must be
  digits only, starting with the country code, with no `+`, spaces or dashes —
  for example `6281234567890`. The box will tell you if it is wrong.
- The **map** on the Contact page only appears once you paste a **Google Maps
  link**. On Google Maps: *Share → Embed a map*, then copy the address inside
  `src="…"`.

### Adding a menu link

In **Site Settings → Menu**, click **Add item** under Menu links, then choose
where it goes:

- **A category of jewellery** — pick from your categories. If you later rename
  that category, the link updates itself.
- **A page on this site** — pick from the list.
- **Another website** — paste the full address.

Leave **What the link says** blank to use the category or page's own name.

If you delete every menu link, the menu goes back to listing your categories
automatically. It never ends up empty.

## 10. Seeing your change before you publish it

Changes you have typed but not published are called a **draft**. Visitors still
see the old version until you press Publish, so you can take your time.

To see how a draft will actually look:

1. Click **Preview** in the top bar of the content manager.
2. The website opens on the right, with your unpublished changes already applied.
3. Edit on the left — the preview updates as you type.

Only you can see this. Visitors continue to see the published version until you
press Publish.

### Jumping straight to the right field

In the preview, hover over any text or photo and a small pencil appears. Click
it and the content manager opens the exact field that produces it. This is the
quickest way to change something when you can see it on the page but do not know
which tab it lives in.

### Previewing a specific page

Open any document and click **Preview**, and it opens the page that document
controls. For a piece of jewellery you get a choice: its own page, or the
catalogue it appears in.

You can also just browse inside the preview pane like a normal website — click
through the menu and the editor follows along.

### If the preview looks stuck

Close the preview and reopen it. If the site still shows old content, you may
have left preview mode switched on in a separate tab; visit
**yoursite.com/api/draft-mode/disable** to return to the normal published site.

---

## 11. Publishing

Click **Publish** at the bottom right. The button is greyed out when there is
nothing new to publish.

If the button will not work, look for a red warning on one of the fields — a
required box is probably empty. Fix it and try again.

---

## 12. Undoing a mistake

**If you have not published yet:** click the **⋮** menu at the bottom and choose
**Discard changes**. Your draft is thrown away and the published version is
untouched.

**If you have already published:** every document keeps a full history.

1. Open the document.
2. Click the **⋮** menu (bottom right) → **Review changes**.
3. Pick a point in time from the list on the right.
4. Click **Restore**.

Nothing is ever really lost, so it is safe to experiment.

**If you deleted a piece by accident**, contact your developer — deleted
documents can usually still be recovered, but not from this screen.

---

## Things you cannot do (on purpose)

- **Delete a page.** Pages can only be edited, never removed — a missing page
  would break the website.
- **Create a second copy of a page** or of Site Settings. There is only ever one
  of each.
- **Create or delete a customer order.** Orders come from real purchases. You can
  update the payment and delivery status, but not invent or remove one.
- **Set an exact pixel size for a photo.** You choose from set options instead,
  so a photo can never come out stretched or break the page layout.
